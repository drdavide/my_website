>>Davide D'Amico

Engineering the world.

Born and raised in Italy, with the passion of travels, music and digital technologies. I try to solve real-life problems with an interdisciplinary approach and lateral thinking, in a sustainable and engineering way. I am an EngD (Engineering Doctorate) student in digital manufacturing under the TES (Through-life Engineering Services) department at the Cranfield University (UK). My research topic is to develop digital twins of through-life degradation for high-value systems.

I hold a B.Sc. and an M.Sc. in Energy Engineering respectively from the University of Padova and the Free University of Bolzano/Bozen both in Italy. I also had two abroad experiences throughout my studies. The first one at the Vienna University of Technology in Austria, where I spent an exchange semester; the second one at the Chiang Mai University in Thailand, where I work for my master's dissertation.

I have also worked in several companies, among all Fraunhofer Italia, where I worked as a junior research engineer in the team of mechatronics and automation engineering; Vimar group S.p.A., where I worked as a junior product manager in the team of strategic product marketing; and General Electric, where I worked as fleet manager in the team of service requisition engineering.
